US Stock Market

AAPL | AMZN | TSLA

Background

In analyzing the US stock market, particularly focusing on Apple Inc. (AAPL), Amazon.com Inc. (AMZN), and Tesla Inc. (TSLA), data visualization provides valuable insights into market trends. The visualization showcases the volatility of each stock through time series charts, illustrating fluctuations in stock prices over a specified period. Additionally, candlestick charts provide a detailed view of daily trading patterns, revealing open, high, low, and closing prices. These visualizations enable investors and analysts to identify patterns, trends, and potential entry or exit points, aiding in strategic decision-making within the dynamic landscape of the US stock market.

Problem Statement

Despite the vast amount of available data on the US stock market, the absence of a comprehensive and visually intuitive analysis tool for major stocks like AAPL, AMZN, and TSLA hinders investors' ability to make informed decisions. The lack of a unified platform offering dynamic visualization of volatility, stock prices, and candlestick patterns presents a challenge in interpreting and understanding market trends. Addressing this gap is crucial to empower investors with a more insightful and user-friendly tool for analyzing the dynamic behavior of these stocks, facilitating more informed investment strategies in the ever-changing landscape of the US stock market."

Objectives

  • Develop a user-friendly and interactive data visualization platform that integrates real-time and historical data for AAPL, AMZN, and TSLA, allowing investors to analyze and compare volatility trends over specific time frames.
  • Implement a robust visualization system that accurately portrays stock price movements through intuitive time series charts, facilitating a comprehensive understanding of the historical performance of AAPL, AMZN, and TSLA for strategic investment decision-making.
  • Create an insightful representation of daily trading patterns using candlestick charts, enhancing the ability of investors to identify key market trends, such as reversals or continuations, thereby empowering them with actionable insights to optimize trading and investment strategies.

Vizualizing Volality

import plotly.graph_objects as go

dataframes = {
	"AAPL": df_AAPL,
	"AMZN": df_AMZN,
	"TSLA": df_TSLA
}
										
colors = {
	"AAPL": "lightblue",
	"AMZN": "orange",
	"TSLA": "darkred"
}
										
# Line plot for Daily Percentage Change
fig1 = go.Figure()
										
for stock, df in dataframes.items():
	fig1.add_trace(go.Scatter(x=df['date'], y=df['change_percent'], mode='lines', name=stock, line=dict(color=colors[stock])))
										
fig1.update_layout(
	title='Daily Percentage Change for AAPL, AMZN, and TSLA',
	xaxis_title='Date',
	yaxis_title='Percentage Change',
	template='plotly_white'
)
fig1.show()
Volality Analysis Results:
  1. The plot indicates different volatility levels for the three stocks. TSLA shows the highest volatility with percentage changes reaching above 20% and below -20%. AAPL appears to be the least volatile, with fewer extreme spikes in daily percentage change.
  2. There is a visible increase in volatility for AMZN around the early 2000s, which could be associated with the dot-com bubble. TSLA shows increased volatility in the more recent years, possibly correlating with its rapid growth and market reactions to news about the company or the automotive industry.
Investment Strategy:
  1. For risk-averse investors, AAPL might be the preferred stock given its lower volatility.
  2. Risk-tolerant investors might prefer TSLA for potential higher returns, accepting the higher volatility.
  3. Diversification across these stocks could mitigate risk while allowing for exposure to the growth potential of the tech sector.
Recommendations:

Investors should consider their risk tolerance, investment horizon, and the overall market conditions when making decisions. It's also important to look at other financial metrics and news that could affect stock performance. Additionally, considering the use of stop-loss orders or options strategies could help manage risk in these volatile stocks.

plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

  4. Apple (AAPL) and Amazon (AMZN) also show significant growth but with less volatility than Tesla.
  5. There are periods where all three stocks seem to rise or fall in tandem, which could indicate market-wide trends or events affecting the tech sector as a whole.
Recommendations:
  1. For investors looking for potentially higher growth with higher risk, Tesla (TSLA) might be of interest due to its rapid and volatile growth pattern.
  2. For those seeking more stable investments, Apple (AAPL) and Amazon (AMZN) might be more suitable as they show strong growth with less volatility.

Vizualizing AAPL Candle Stick

# Creating a subset with the last 250 records
df_subset = df_AAPL[-250:].copy()
										
# Calculate moving averages
df_subset['ma10'] = df_subset['close'].rolling(window=10).mean()
df_subset['ma20'] = df_subset['close'].rolling(window=20).mean()
										
# Determine the volume color
df_subset['color'] = df_subset.apply(lambda row: 'green' if row['close'] >= row['open'] else 'red', axis=1)
										
# Trace for candlestick
trace1 = go.Candlestick(
	x=df_subset['date'],
	open=df_subset['open'],
	high=df_subset['high'],
	low=df_subset['low'],
	close=df_subset['close'],
	name='Price',
	yaxis='y1'
)
										
# Trace for volume
trace2 = go.Bar(
	x=df_subset['date'],
	y=df_subset['volume'],
	marker=dict(color=df_subset['color']),
	name='Volume',
	yaxis='y2'
)
										
# Trace for the 20-day moving average of volume
trace3 = go.Scatter(
	x=df_subset['date'],
	y=df_subset['avg_vol_20d'],
	mode='lines',
	line=dict(color='grey', width=1, dash='dash'),
	name='20-day Volume MA',
	yaxis='y2'
)
										
# Trace for the 10-day moving average of price
trace4 = go.Scatter(
	x=df_subset['date'],
	y=df_subset['ma10'],
	mode='lines',
	line=dict(color='royalblue', width=0.7, dash='dash'),
	name='10-day MA',
	yaxis='y1'
)
										
# Trace for the 20-day moving average of price
trace5 = go.Scatter(
	x=df_subset['date'],
	y=df_subset['ma20'],
	mode='lines',
	line=dict(color='blue', width=0.7, dash='dash'),
	name='20-day MA',
	yaxis='y1'
)
										
# Defining the layout
layout = go.Layout(
	title={'text': 'AAPL Candlestick Chart with Plotly', 'x': 0.5, 'y':0.9},
	xaxis=dict(title='Date'),
	yaxis=dict(title='Price ($)', domain=[0.35, 1]),
	yaxis2=dict(title='Volume', domain=[0, 0.3], side='left'),
	xaxis_rangeslider_visible=False,
	barmode='overlay',
	legend=dict(orientation="h", yanchor="bottom", y=-0.15, xanchor="center", x=0.5)
)
										
# Creating and showing the figure
fig = go.Figure(data=[trace1, trace2, trace3, trace4, trace5], layout=layout)
fig.show()
AAPL Candle Stick Results:
  1. The candlestick chart shows the price movement of AAPL stock over time, from September 2022 to July 2023. Each candlestick represents the opening, closing, high, and low prices for a given time period.
  2. The chart includes three moving averages: a 10-day MA (moving average), a 20-day MA, and a 20-day volume MA. These are typically used to smooth out price data to identify the trend direction.
  3. The 10-day MA (dotted line) and the 20-day MA (dashed line) are close to each other, suggesting that the short-term and medium-term trends are aligned.
  4. The volume bars represent the number of shares traded during a given time period, with green bars indicating a closing price higher than the opening price (bullish sentiment), and red bars indicating a closing price lower than the opening price (bearish sentiment).
  5. The 20-day volume MA (solid line within the volume bars) helps to understand the average trading volume over the past 20 days.
Actionable Insight:
  1. The overall trend from September 2022 to July 2023 is upward, as indicated by the general increase in the stock price and the position of the moving averages.
  2. Periods of high trading volume, especially when accompanied by large price movements, can indicate stronger conviction in the price movement.
  3. The moving averages can act as support and resistance levels. If the stock price dips towards a moving average, it might find support there and bounce back.
Recommendations:
  1. If the current price is above the moving averages and the moving averages are trending upwards, it may be considered a bullish signal, suggesting a potential buying opportunity.
  2. If the price falls below the moving averages, it could be a sign that the trend is weakening, and investors might want to wait for further confirmation before making a decision.
  3. Investors should also consider other factors such as company fundamentals, market conditions, and news events when making investment decisions.
