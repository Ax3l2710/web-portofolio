Customer Retention

| Business Acumen | Phyton | Cohort Analysis | Heat Map |

Background

PADI (Professional Association of Diving Instructor) UMKM recognizes a significant untapped potential in leveraging its customer base and aims to capitalize on this opportunity through strategic initiatives. To achieve this, the organization is planning to implement diverse approaches tailored to different customer segments by conducting comprehensive customer segmentation analysis. The goal is to gain a deeper understanding of each segment's unique characteristics and behaviors, allowing PADI UMKM to enhance overall business performance through targeted strategies.

In addition to customer-focused efforts, PADI UMKM is committed to supporting its sellers effectively. The organization acknowledges the importance of understanding the characteristics of its sellers to provide them with improved support. By gaining insights into the needs and challenges faced by sellers, PADI UMKM aims to develop strategies that facilitate better collaboration and ultimately contribute to the success of both the organization and its sellers. This dual-focus approach, involving both customer segmentation analysis and seller support initiatives, reflects PADI UMKM's commitment to a holistic and strategic approach for sustainable growth and success in the market.

Problem Statement

The current marketing strategy at PADI UMKM lacks a systematic approach to understanding and leveraging customer behavior, resulting in a missed opportunity for business growth. The absence of customer segmentation analysis has led to insufficient insights into the diverse characteristics and preferences of our customer base.

Method

  1. Cohort Analysis
  2. Heat Map

Customer Retention Rate Results:

  1. The retention rates generally decrease over time for each cohort.
  2. The first cohort (2022-01) starts with a 100% retention rate, as expected, and shows a significant drop to 52.6% in the second month, followed by a gradual decline to 73% by the twelfth month.
  3. Subsequent cohorts also start with 100% retention in their first month and show a similar trend of sharp decline in the second month, with varying degrees of decrease in the following months.
  4. The retention rates for later months are generally lower, indicating a decrease in customer retention as time progresses.
  5. The color gradient from dark blue to light green indicates higher to lower retention rates, respectively.

Insights:

  1. The sharp decline from the first to the second month across all cohorts suggests that customer engagement initiatives could be most impactful if implemented early in the customer lifecycle.
  2. The varying retention rates in the later months across cohorts suggest that some months may have specific factors affecting retention, which could be investigated further.

Recommendations:

  1. Investigate the causes of the sharp decline after the first month to develop targeted strategies for improving the second-month retention.
  2. Analyze customer behavior, feedback, and transaction patterns to identify potential reasons for churn.
  3. Implement early engagement strategies, such as onboarding programs, customer education, and initial promotions, to boost retention in the early stages.
  4. Consider personalized retention campaigns for customers who have been with the company for longer periods, as their retention rates tend to dwindle over time.
  5. Regularly track and analyze retention metrics to identify trends and develop timely interventions.
